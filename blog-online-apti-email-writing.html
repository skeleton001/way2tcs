Email Writing: Do's and Don'ts

Blog / Online Apti

Hello folks.. When my friend (pirate) told me about this site, I asked him for some space to interact with you people so that you can learn from the biggest mistake which I committed in TCS exam. So talking about writing an email, though it seems most simple job, yet the toughest in reality. You would be given a question with some phrases, using these you have to frame an email. Subject may be either formal or informal. You have to read the question, write an email with proper format and SAVE it time to time in 10 minutes, Only!!!

KISS... (Keep It Short n Simple)

Now you may think, what's so difficult in it. So let me tell you that I appeared for TCS Off campus drive twice and committed the same mistake again. For the first time I didn't know that the email is to be saved (there are two sperate buttons: SAVE n SUBMIT) and I thought, as the 10 mins get over, the email will be directly submitted just like the answers marked in analytical section get submitted as the time counter becomes zero. And the second time I could not manage to read the question, write an email containing all the phases and to save it, all in 10 mins. Actually it's my problem that as I see the time is running, I become panic more and more (you may say fear of time). This makes me forget everything. And when the time counter became zero, I noticed under verbal section, number of questions answered were ZERO. As soon as the verbal section got submitted, I came to know that I committed the same mistake again i.e. I didn't hit SAVE Button even a single time in last 10 mins. Whereas, at both the times I solved analytical section very nicely. But unfortunately, just because I didn't save my email, I lost TCS twice (costs a lot). This clearly means, even if you write excellent email with proper format, it's of no use until you SAVE it or atleast SUBMIT it before counter stopped. It is not at all necessary to save the email only after it's completion. Keep on SAVING it time to time, so that even if you are not in the condition to finish writing, at least the text you entered before last time you hit SAVE BUTTON will be considered. In this case you may see, number of questions attempted under verbal section will be less than 1 but definitely not zero.

Here are some tips for you. Let's see an example:

Question: As the secretary of your residential society, Royal Regency. Write an email to inspector of local Police station, Mr. Manoj Sharma, informing him about miscreants who ride their bikes rashly every evening outside your society. Sign the email as Shashikant Khare
residential area - ride - rashly - children - play - elderly - walk - grocery shop - across the road - dangerous - accidents - nuisance - action - immediately.

The opening of the professional mail should start with a greeting to the addressee as mentioned in the question like ‘Hello/ Dear XYZ (addressee)’. Always use the last name (I prefer) to address and not full name. In case of above example, the opening should be like this-

Dear Mr. Sharma

After the line of greeting, you can directly jump on main contents of the mail. Make sure that you use all the phrases provided in the question. You can use your own phrases too, but don’t waste much time in it. Try to keep it short and simple with the use of simple English, correct grammar and punctuations. At last signing is to be done. For a formal email, you may sign as 'Thanks and regards' in the next line ABC (sender)’, followed by the designation if mentioned in the question. Or you may write ‘Thanking you/Thanking you in anticipation'. followed by 'Yours sincerely in the next line ABC (sender)’. Whereas you may sign informal email as, ‘Love and regards in the next line ABC (sender)’. In case of above example, the signing should be like this-

Thanks and regards
Shashikant Khare
Secretary, Royal Regency

Addressing and signing should be necessarily done as mentioned in the question given. If you write the mail following the sequence of format and you are in short of time, you may miss the singing which carries marks. So, I’ll suggest you to address and sign the mail at very beginning and then fill up the contents in between them.

NOTE: If you are left with last 5 sec and you have to write only couple of words to finish your mail, then I'll suggest that first SAVE the mail and then if time permits complete your write-up. Just like tie your supplement first and then write :p
